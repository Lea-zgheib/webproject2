The internet is involved in nearly every aspect of our lives — whether it's for education, professional tasks, shopping, or keeping in contact with friends. However, going online carries certain dangers. Cyber threats such as phishing scams, malware, data breaches, and identity theft are increasingly prevalent and can lead to significant issues, including the loss of personal information and financial harm.

1. Common Risks on the Web

  • Phishing: Fake emails or websites trick users into sharing personal info.
  • Malware: Harmful software that steals data or slows devices.
  • Data Breaches: Hackers steal sensitive user information.
  • Identity Theft: Criminals use your personal info for fraud.
  • Weak Passwords: Easy or reused passwords are risky.
  • Unsafe Wi-Fi: Hackers can spy on open network connections.

2. Safe Practices to Protect Yourself

  • Use Strong Passwords: Different and complex passwords for each account.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Adds an extra layer of protection.
  • Keep Software Updated: Updates fix vulnerabilities.
  • Be Wary of Phishing: Avoid suspicious links or attachments.
  • Use Secure Connections: Always prefer HTTPS or VPN on public Wi-Fi.
  • Back Up Data Regularly: Prevent data loss from attacks.
  • Use Antivirus & Firewalls: Detect and block harmful activities.
